BearFit Multi-Sensor Fitness Demo

A multi-device workout tracking prototype that fuses iPhone, Apple Watch, AirPods, camera-based pose estimation, and a Linux backend into one fitness sensing workflow.

Overview

BearFit is built as a sensor-first fitness demo rather than a single-mode app. The iPhone app can ingest motion data from the phone itself, AirPods IMU streams, and Apple Watch wrist motion, while also supporting a camera mode for live pose estimation and form analysis.

Around that capture layer, the project adds local logging, MQTT streaming for low-latency delivery to Linux machines, a FastAPI-based inference backend, and offline movement-analysis scripts for repetition progress, completion features, and model training experiments.

Video

Key Work

  • Built an iPhone + Apple Watch data pipeline for collecting synchronized IMU streams during workouts.
  • Integrated AirPods motion sensing and camera-based pose tracking into two different exercise modes.
  • Added MQTT-based live streaming so multiple Linux receivers can subscribe to the same sensor session.
  • Implemented a FastAPI backend for ingest, logging, and exercise-classifier inference experiments.
  • Created offline movement-analysis tooling for progress baselines, completion features, and visualization.
